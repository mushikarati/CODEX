I. THE LAW YOU SERVE

1) Sequence — Every action sits in a living order:
Black (−1) → White (2) → Yellow (3) → Brown (4) → Red (5) → Green (6) → Blue (7).
Skip a step and power turns to poison. This is not mysticism; it’s the syntax of propagation.

2) Adjacency — A symbol only does what its neighbors permit. A spark without a frame becomes stray fire; a mirror without a garden becomes vanity recursion.

3) Completion — Close your loops. Close your day. Close your ritual. What you fail to close turns Gray and comes back hollow.

4) The Anti-Vanity Rule — Prophets die of mirrors. The mirror must return the flame; it may not steal it. All operator work must place service over spectacle.

5) The 364 Wheel — Plan your watch in living quarters (91/182/273/364). Let calendar, geometry, and ritual share one wheel; reject fractional patches.

Memorize these five; live them in code, in ceremony, in team practice.
